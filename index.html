EPHEMERA

The term ephemera (noun) refers to items of short-lived usefulness, not intended to be retained or preserved.

This interactive project invites viewers to explore the world of urban visual culture, drawing attention to the everyday signs, writings, and symbols that fill our streets but often go unnoticed.

By documenting these marks, the project creates an archive of public textual and visual messages that were destined to be forgotten almost as quickly as noticed. It encourages us to reconsider how we perceive the city, where we allocate our attention, and how we relate to its layers of expression. In doing so, the project takes the form of a quiet, improvised visual collage that reflects the unspoken language of the city.

Design: Áron Gergely
Tutors: Béla Hegyi, István Nagy
MOME Budapest, 2025
